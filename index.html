★ THE SYNTHESIS ★

Karl Marx

1818–1883 · with Friedrich Engels (1820–1895)

Marx synthesized German philosophy (Hegel's dialectics), French socialism (utopian visions of a just society), and English political economy (Smith & Ricardo's labor theory of value) into a unified, revolutionary framework: historical materialism. He argued that history is driven by class struggle rooted in the material conditions of production, that capitalism generates its own gravediggers in the proletariat, and that communism is not an ideal to be realized but the real movement abolishing the present state of things.

KEY: The Communist Manifesto (1848), Grundrisse (1857–58), Capital Vol. I (1867), The Eighteenth Brumaire (1852), Theses on Feuerbach (1845), The German Ideology (1846)
